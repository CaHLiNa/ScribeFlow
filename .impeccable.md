## Design Context

### Users

Altals serves people who spend long stretches drafting and revising project-based documents on desktop: technical writers, researchers, students, and knowledge workers editing Markdown, LaTeX, and Typst. Their core job is to stay focused on writing, structure, preview, and compile feedback without being distracted by heavy IDE chrome.

### Brand Personality

Quiet, precise, trustworthy.
The interface should feel calm, deliberate, and professionally editorial rather than clever, chatty, or overly technical. It should reduce pressure and visual noise while still feeling capable.

### Aesthetic Direction

The target direction is a quiet minimal writing tool with a hint of paper-like warmth. Codex is a useful reference for restraint, spacing, and composure, but Altals should remain document-first rather than chat-first. The anti-reference is VS Code or a general-purpose IDE: too many rails, hard separators, dense status bars, and tool-centric framing.

### Design Principles

1. Let the document surface be the visual center of gravity.
2. Prefer calm panels and soft grouping over hard chrome and dense toolbars.
3. Make controls feel secondary and available, not constantly demanding attention.
4. Use restrained typography, neutral surfaces, and gentle contrast before adding accent color.
5. Keep the product feeling like a professional writing studio, not a developer workstation.
